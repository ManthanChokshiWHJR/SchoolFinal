Interschool Football competetion
Won second prize in interschool Football Competetion

Considered to be the oldest national school football tournament in India, the Subroto Cup is named after Indian Air Force Air Marshal Subroto Mukerjee. He was the first Indian Commander-in-Chief of the Indian Air Force. The Subroto Cup was introduced to promote and encourage the game of football at the grassroots level in the country. The prestigious inter-school football tournament has been held annually since 1960. Around 50 school teams participated in the inaugural edition. It has been played in two age groups, sub-Junior (under 14 years) and Junior (under 17 years) since 1998.

What's New

  • New School Canteen

    There are several aspects that can be improved in a school canteen to enhance the overall experience for students. First and foremost, the quality and variety of food options should be a priority. Offering nutritious and diverse menu items that cater to different dietary preferences and restrictions can promote healthier eating habits among students. Additionally, schools can work on improving the overall cleanliness and hygiene of the canteen, ensuring that food preparation and serving areas are spotless and meet health and safety standards. Furthermore, the efficiency of service can be enhanced. Long waiting times can discourage students from eating at the canteen, so streamlining the ordering and payment process can significantly improve the overall experience. It's also important to consider the pricing of food items to make them more affordable for all students, regardless of their economic backgrounds. Another key improvement could involve engaging students in the decision-making process by seeking their input on menu choices and canteen improvements. This not only empowers students but also helps in tailoring the canteen offerings to their preferences. In conclusion, a school canteen should prioritize food quality, variety, cleanliness, efficiency, affordability, and student involvement to create a more positive and inclusive dining environment.

  • Implemented online attendence management system

    Efficiency: Online attendance management systems automate the process of recording attendance, saving time and reducing administrative workload. This allows staff to focus on more important tasks. Accuracy: These systems reduce the risk of manual errors in attendance tracking. They can use biometrics, RFID, or other technologies to accurately record attendance. Real-time Tracking: Online systems provide real-time attendance data, allowing supervisors and HR to monitor attendance instantly. This can help in addressing attendance issues promptly. Data Analysis: Data collected by these systems can be analyzed to identify attendance patterns, trends, and anomalies. This can be useful for making data-driven decisions. Cost Savings: Reduced paperwork and administrative work can result in cost savings. It eliminates the need for physical attendance registers and related paperwork. Employee Accountability: The transparency of online attendance systems can promote accountability among employees. They are aware that their attendance is being tracked, which can deter absenteeism and tardiness. Flexibility: Online systems often offer features like remote attendance tracking, which can be beneficial for organizations with remote or flexible work arrangements. Integration: Many attendance management systems can be integrated with other HR and payroll software, streamlining various HR processes. Security: These systems often come with security features to protect attendance data, ensuring it is not tampered with or accessed by unauthorized individuals. Compliance: Such systems can assist in ensuring compliance with labor laws and regulations by accurately recording work hours and overtime. Improved Communication: Some systems offer features like notifications and alerts, which can improve communication between management and employees regarding attendance-related matters. Reduced Fraud: Biometric authentication or RFID cards can help reduce the possibility of buddy punching or other fraudulent attendance practices. Environmental Impact: Online attendance systems promote a paperless approach, contributing to a reduction in an organization's environmental footprint. Employee Self-service: Many systems allow employees to manage their own attendance, request time off, and view their attendance records, reducing HR's workload. Scalability: These systems can be easily scaled to accommodate the changing needs of an organization as it grows. Accessibility: Attendance data can be accessed from anywhere with an internet connection, making it convenient for remote or geographically dispersed teams.
